Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda (1863–1902) was a Hindu monk, philosopher, and key figure in introducing Indian spirituality and Vedanta to the Western world. He was a disciple of Sri Ramakrishna and played a major role in the revival of Hinduism in India.

Key Contributions & Teachings:

  • Chicago Speech (1893): At the World’s Parliament of Religions, he introduced Hindu philosophy, beginning with "Sisters and Brothers of America," which received a standing ovation.
  • Vedanta & Yoga: He popularized the concepts of Vedanta and Yoga in India and the West.
  • Service to Humanity: He emphasized “Daridra Narayana Seva” (serving the poor as God).
  • Ramakrishna Mission (1897): He established this organization for social service, education, and spiritual upliftment.
  • Nation-Building: He inspired Indian youth with his teachings on strength, self-confidence, and patriotism.

    • Famous Quotes:

  • "Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached."
  • "You cannot believe in God until you believe in yourself."
  • "Strength is life, weakness is death."
